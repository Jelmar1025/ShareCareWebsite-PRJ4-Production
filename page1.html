the journey of maria

Are you tired of playing it safe and following the expected path? Then, you'll be inspired by the story of Maria, the fearless expat who traded textbooks for tattoo needles and never looked back.

Maria had always dreamed of becoming an artist. She spent most of her childhood sketching and painting, and her passion only grew as she got older. She knew she wanted to pursue a career in art, but as she finished high school, she realized that she needed a more stable career. That's why she decided to move abroad to study graphic design in the Netherlands.

In her first year abroad, Maria met some amazing people, including a tattoo artist named Alex. Alex was passionate about his work, and Maria was intrigued by his artistry. She started spending time at his studio, watching him work and learning about his craft.

One day, Alex offered Maria a start-up at his studio. He saw potential in her work, and he believed that she had the talent to become a great tattoo artist. She started working at the studio part-time, practicing her skills and learning from Alex.

As the months passed, Maria fell in love with the art of tattooing. She loved the creative freedom it gave her, and she loved the satisfaction of seeing her work come to life on someone's skin. She realized that this was what she had been missing in her graphic design studies. She had been pursuing a career that was stable and safe, but it wasn't fulfilling her artistic passions.

One year later, Maria made the difficult decision to give up her studies and pursue her own business as a tattoo artist. It was a risky move, but she knew that it was the right decision for her. She opened her own studio, and she was happier than ever before.

Maria's journey is a reminder that life is about breaking boundaries and chasing your dreams. It's about taking risks, embracing the unexpected, and following your passions, no matter where they take you. As expats, we've already taken the first step towards chasing our dreams by moving abroad. We've broken free from the limitations of our hometowns and embraced new cultures, languages, and ways of living.

But the journey doesn't stop there. It's up to us to keep pushing ourselves, exploring new paths, and pursuing our passions. So, whether you're an aspiring artist, a budding entrepreneur, or simply someone who wants to try something new, take a page out of Maria's book and go for it. You never know where your passions will take you.

As expats, we're already breaking boundaries every day, so why not take it a step further and chase our wildest dreams? Life is too short to play it safe, so let's take a leap of faith and see where it leads us. Who knows, we might just surprise ourselves.

Top 10 things to do in the netherlands: 

The Netherlands is a small country that packs a big punch when it comes to culture, history, and natural beauty. If you're an expat living in the Netherlands, there's so much to explore beyond the borders of your city. Here are 10 must-see destinations and experiences that will make your time in the Netherlands unforgettable.

Visit the Rijksmuseum in Amsterdam:
The Rijksmuseum is one of the most famous museums in the world and home to some of the most iconic artworks of Dutch history, such as Rembrandt's "The Night Watch." Spend a day marveling at the masterpieces that tell the story of the Netherlands' rich cultural heritage.

Explore the canals of Utrecht:
Utrecht is a charming city with a medieval heart, and the best way to explore it is by boat. Take a canal tour and see the city from a different perspective, while learning about its fascinating history.

Experience the Keukenhof Gardens:
The Keukenhof Gardens are a stunning display of the Netherlands' world-famous tulips. Visit during the spring and be awed by the millions of colorful flowers that bloom every year, creating a breathtaking landscape.

Discover the historical city of Maastricht:
Maastricht is a historic city located in the south of the Netherlands. It boasts an impressive Roman history and has a vibrant cultural scene. Take a walk through its quaint streets and explore the old fortifications, churches, and castles that bear witness to its rich history.

Go biking in the Veluwe:
The Veluwe is the largest national park in the Netherlands and a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. Rent a bike and explore the rolling hills, lush forests, and picturesque towns that make up this stunning natural landscape.

Visit the Wadden Islands:
The Wadden Islands are a chain of five islands located in the north of the Netherlands. Each island has its unique character and charm, with sandy beaches, quaint villages, and diverse wildlife. Take a ferry to one of the islands and spend the day soaking up the sun and sea air.

Take a stroll through Rotterdam:
Rotterdam is a modern and innovative city that offers a striking contrast to the traditional Dutch towns. Walk around the city and marvel at the stunning architecture, such as the iconic Cube Houses or the impressive Erasmus Bridge.

Experience the Frisian lakes:
The Frisian lakes are a network of lakes located in the northern part of the Netherlands. It's the perfect destination for water sports enthusiasts, such as sailing, windsurfing, and kayaking. Rent a boat and explore the pristine waters and quaint villages that surround the lakes.

Attend a concert at the TivoliVredenburg in Utrecht:
TivoliVredenburg is one of the largest music venues in the Netherlands, with a diverse program that caters to all tastes. Attend a concert and experience the vibrant Dutch music scene in a world-class setting.

Walk the streets of The Hague:
The Hague is the political capital of the Netherlands and home to many international organizations. It's also a vibrant city with a beach and a lively cultural scene. Take a walk through the city and visit the iconic Peace Palace, the Binnenhof, and the many museums and galleries that make The Hague an art lover's paradise.

In conclusion, the Netherlands offers a wealth of cultural, historical, and natural attractions that are waiting to be explored. Whether you're looking for outdoor adventures, artistic inspiration, or just a taste of Dutch culture, there's something for everyone in this small but mighty country.

for Breda

Breda, a quaint city in the south of the Netherlands, is a hidden gem that every expat should explore. With its rich history, lively culture, and stunning architecture, there's no shortage of things to do in Breda. Here are 10 must-see places and experiences that will make your visit to this charming city one to remember.

Visit Grote Kerk:
Grote Kerk is a towering gothic church that dominates the city skyline. Its impressive interior features intricate stone carvings, stained-glass windows, and a breathtaking organ. Take a guided tour to learn about the church's history and architecture, or attend a classical concert for a truly memorable experience.

Explore the Begijnhof:
The Begijnhof is a tranquil courtyard surrounded by historic houses and gardens. This hidden gem offers a glimpse into the city's past and provides a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of daily life. Take a stroll through the gardens and marvel at the well-preserved architecture.

Enjoy a beer at the Breda Brewery:
Breda Brewery has been producing beer for over 300 years, and its rich history is reflected in the flavors of its brews. Take a tour of the brewery to learn about the brewing process and sample some of their unique beers. For an extra special experience, book a beer-pairing dinner and taste the perfect combination of food and beer.

Visit the Stedelijk Museum Breda:
The Stedelijk Museum Breda is a contemporary art museum located in a former military barracks. It features a wide variety of exhibitions, ranging from modern art to historical collections. Whether you're an art enthusiast or just looking for something new, the Stedelijk Museum Breda is a must-see attraction.

Take a walk in Valkenberg Park:
Valkenberg Park is a sprawling green oasis in the heart of the city. It's perfect for a picnic, a jog, or just a leisurely stroll. The park also features a beautiful castle and a small zoo, making it a great destination for families.

Discover the Breda Castle:
Breda Castle is a stunning example of Dutch Renaissance architecture. The castle's history dates back to the 12th century and it has been the residence of the Dutch Royal Family for centuries. Take a guided tour of the castle and learn about its fascinating history.

Shop in the historic city center:
Breda's city center is filled with charming shops and boutiques. From designer clothing to handmade crafts, there's something for everyone. Take a leisurely stroll down the narrow streets and discover the hidden gems that await.

Attend a festival or event:
Breda is known for its lively festivals and events. From the annual Carnival celebration to the Jazz Festival, there's always something going on in this vibrant city. Check the calendar to see what's happening during your visit and join in the fun.

Explore the Breda Mastbos:
The Breda Mastbos is a beautiful forest that offers miles of hiking and biking trails. With its serene lakes, rolling hills, and diverse wildlife, it's the perfect destination for nature lovers. Pack a picnic and spend the day exploring this idyllic forest.

Take a boat tour on the Mark river:
The Mark river runs through the heart of Breda, and taking a boat tour is the perfect way to explore the city from a different perspective. Glide along the peaceful waterways and admire the stunning architecture and landmarks from the water.

In conclusion, Breda offers an abundance of cultural and natural attractions that are perfect for expats looking to discover more of the Netherlands. From exploring historic landmarks to indulging in local cuisine, there's something for everyone to enjoy in their free time.
